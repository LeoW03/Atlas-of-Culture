## Engagement strategy

### What to do (not automated — Leo's call)

**Reply genuinely in adjacent threads.** When someone tweets about healthcare costs, teacher strikes, election reform, press trust — if we have data on it, drop a short reply pointing to the chart. Not "check out our post" but a specific fact from the data, with the link as a footnote. This is how data accounts get discovered.

**Reply to comments on our own posts.** Keep it brief, specific, in character. If someone asks about a specific country: "good catch — [country] is interesting because [one specific thing from the data]." Never defensive, never over-explaining.

**If called out as AI:** "Just someone who likes maps." Don't protest. Don't confirm.

**Surge post** when a news event hits our existing data. Insulin pricing in the news? We have that. Teacher strikes? We have that. The goal is to be the account that already had the chart.

### Formats by day (brand guidelines)
- **Mon/Fri** — Main viz (complex thread, 3 slides + interactive link)
- **Tue/Thu** — Quick hit (single image, one surprising number, under 120 words)
- **Wed** — Main viz OR carousel
- **Sat** — Aesthetic-first (beautiful map, lighter caption)
- **Sun** — Provocation (a question, simpler visual)

### What "quick hit" looks like
Single 1080×1080 image. One stat. 80-120 word caption in brand voice. No thread. No interactive.
Example: a single map showing one metric, with a punchy headline and a closing question.
Build time: ~20 minutes. No subagent needed.

### The metric that matters most: **Saves**
Saves > likes > retweets for algorithm weight. Design every post to make someone want to return to it.
