Where Teachers Are Paid Like Professionals
Luxembourg pays teachers 198% of the average salary. The US pays 64%. We mapped teacher pay ratios against PISA scores across 40 countries.
The Insulin Price Gap
The same vial of insulin. Germany pays $11. The US pays $98.70. A global map of pharmaceutical pricing inequality.
Free Press, Mistrusted Press
Finland ranks 1st in press freedom. 69% of Finns trust the media. The US ranks 55th. 32% of Americans do. These are not the same problem.
Less School, Better Scores
Finland: 593 hours of school per year. The US: 1,016 hours. Finland scores near the top. The US middling. More hours, worse outcomes.
The Democracy Satisfaction Paradox
Switzerland: 45% voter turnout. 83% satisfied with democracy. The US: 55% turnout. 27% satisfied. Turnout doesn't explain satisfaction.
The Slowdown
The US Congress passed 906 laws in 1947. In 2024, it passed ~100. We mapped legislative output across six democracies from 1950 to today.
Billionaire Density
The US has 813 billionaires. China has 473. India has 200. The US alone has more than China and India combined.
Rent Burden
New York renters spend 68% of median income on rent. Helsinki renters spend 23%. Every city above the 30% stress threshold, mapped.
Vacation Days
France mandates 36 paid vacation days per year. The United States mandates 0. The only OECD country with no legal minimum.
Women in Parliament
Rwanda's parliament is 61% women. Sweden's is 46%. The US is 29%. The map doesn't look how you'd expect.
The Carbon Tax Experiment
Sweden introduced a carbon tax in 1991 at $27/tonne. Today it's $130. GDP kept growing. Emissions kept falling. What happened everywhere else.
Where Languages Go to Die
Of the 7,000 languages spoken today, half will be silent by 2100. A map of language extinction — where, why, and who's left.
The Antidepressant Paradox
Iceland takes more antidepressants per capita than almost anywhere. It's also one of the happiest countries on earth. The correlation you'd expect isn't there.
Who Owns the Night
Light pollution mapped from satellite data. The Korean Peninsula tells the sharpest story: South Korea at night vs North Korea.
The Untaxed Wealth
An estimated $13 trillion in private wealth sits in offshore accounts. A map of where it hides and which countries pay the price.
Where Children Are
The median age in Niger is 15. In Japan, it's 49. A global map of demographic divergence — and what it means for the next 50 years.
The Aid Paradox
Foreign aid and governance quality show almost no correlation. Countries that receive the most aid are not becoming better governed. Why?
Passport Power Gap
Japanese passport: 193 countries visa-free. Afghan passport: 26. That's not a travel inconvenience — it's a map of who gets to move freely.
The Housing Unaffordability Surge
In 1990, a Vancouver home cost 5× the median income. Today: 13×. Sydney: 14×. Tokyo: 6×. One city did something different.
The Sleep Deprivation Index
Japan averages 6h 35m of sleep per night — least in the OECD. There's a word for it: inemuri. Sleeping at work is a sign of dedication.
The Maternal Mortality Gap
The US maternal mortality rate is 23 per 100,000 — same as China, higher than Kazakhstan. The only wealthy country where it's been rising.
