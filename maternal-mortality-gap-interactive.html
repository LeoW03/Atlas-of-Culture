The race gap compounds the geography gap.
Black women in the US die in childbirth at 3× the rate of white women — a disparity that persists regardless of income, education, or insurance status. In states like Georgia and Mississippi, this gap is even wider.
Slide 03 · Trend 2000–2020

Going the Wrong Direction

While peers improved, the US maternal mortality rate more than doubled since 2000. This is not a measurement artifact — it reflects a real public health failure.

Note: A 2003 US coding change added a pregnancy checkbox to death certificates, causing an apparent jump. But even controlling for this, the trend is real and worsening. Peer nations — using similar methodologies — continued to improve.

Caveats & Methodology

  • Maternal mortality definitions vary slightly by country — some include deaths within 42 days of delivery, others up to 1 year.
  • US figures have historically been affected by improved death certificate coding, which may inflate apparent trend increases.
