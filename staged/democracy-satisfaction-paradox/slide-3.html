3 of 3 · The Swiss model
4–6
Switzerland votes on referendums 4–6 times a year.
Not just elections. On specific laws, spending decisions, constitutional amendments. If you don't like something, you can put it to a vote. That's a structurally different relationship with democracy — and it shows in satisfaction data.
★
Compulsory voting inflates turnout, not satisfaction.
Australia, Belgium, Brazil mandate voting. Their turnout is 80–90%. Their satisfaction rates are middling. Being forced to vote doesn't make the system feel more responsive. Switzerland's low turnout is voluntary — and still produces the world's highest democratic satisfaction.
30%
The US votes more than France. Trusts democracy less.
France has 48% turnout. The US has 55%. France scores higher on democratic satisfaction. Turnout doesn't explain the American disenchantment — polarization, institutional dysfunction, and the sense that elections don't change policy outcomes do.
