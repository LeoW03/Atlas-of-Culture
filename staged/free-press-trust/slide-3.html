More free than 130 countries. Less trusted than most of them.
The US has more structural press freedom than Lithuania, Namibia, or Timor-Leste. What it lacks is public belief in the institution.

Local news collapsed. Trust followed.
The US lost roughly 1,800 local newspapers since 2004. What replaced them was national partisan media — and partisan media is precisely what people distrust most.

Americans who actively avoid news
The paradox: a free press nobody reads.
The Reuters Institute finds 41% of Americans now actively avoid news — the highest rate in the study. The problem isn't censorship. It's collapse of trust in the institution itself.
