1921
The patent sold for one dollar.
Frederick Banting discovered insulin and sold the patent to the University of Toronto for $1. His reasoning: no one should profit from a medicine people need to survive.
0
Today
Every wealthy nation negotiates.
The US doesn't.
Germany, France, Japan, and Canada all have national price negotiation for drugs. The US has no equivalent for most medications. Manufacturers set the price. Insurers pay it. Patients absorb what's left.
9M
The consequence
9 million Americans ration their insulin.
Skipping doses. Cutting vials in half. Driving to Canada. Some don't make it. The molecule hasn't changed. The market has.
