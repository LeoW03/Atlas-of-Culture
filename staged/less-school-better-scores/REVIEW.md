# Less School, Better Scores
**Slug:** less-school-better-scores | **Status:** AWAITING SIGN-OFF

## The angle
Finnish students spend 593 hours/year in class — fewest in the OECD. American students spend 1,016 hours. Finland scores near the top on PISA. The US scores below average for its wealth level. Hours of instruction correlate almost nothing with outcomes. What does: teacher quality, curriculum depth, equity of access.
