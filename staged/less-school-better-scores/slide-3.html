Teaching is more selective than medicine.
Finland rejects 9 in 10 applicants to teacher training programs. The curriculum was redesigned around depth, not coverage. Hours were cut. The profession became prestigious.

Time in class ≠ time learning.
South Korean students attend school and then tutoring academies (hagwons) for several more hours. Japanese students benefit from a culture of focused attention. Neither shows up in the instructional hours count — which only measures official classroom time.

The US spends the most hours. Gets middling results.
The US has more instructional time than almost any OECD country. It also has some of the lowest teacher selectivity, widest achievement gaps between rich and poor districts, and one of the largest variances in school quality. Hours are not the constraint.
