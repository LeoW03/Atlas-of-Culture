== TWEET 1 (with slide-1.png: map) ==

Luxembourg pays teachers 198% of the average salary. The US pays 64%.

That gap — not just the number, but what it signals about how a society values the profession — shows up directly in outcomes.

A thread. 🧵

== TWEET 2 (with slide-2.png: scatter) ==

The correlation between teacher pay and PISA scores is real.

But the outliers are more interesting. Singapore: top of the world. Poland: low pay, strong outcomes. Luxembourg: highest pay, middle results.

Pay is one signal. Status is the actual variable.

== TWEET 3 (with slide-3.png: story) ==

Finland accepts fewer than 10% of teacher training applicants. Singapore recruits from the top third of graduates.

The US accepts the majority of applicants. The signal sent: this is not a prestige career.

The data reflects that signal back.
