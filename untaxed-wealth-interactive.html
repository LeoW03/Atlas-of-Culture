The Untaxed Wealth

"The Cayman Islands has a GDP of $6 billion. It holds an estimated $3 trillion in offshore wealth. That's not a country — it's a keyhole."

Data Sources

  • EU Tax Observatory — Global Tax Evasion Report 2024 — Estimates effective tax rates on billionaire wealth across countries, finding billionaires pay 0–0.5% of their wealth annually in taxes; covers 2023–2024 data.
  • World Inequality Database (WID.world) — Tracks top wealth shares and pre-tax income concentration by country using tax records, surveys, and national accounts; data through 2023.
  • ProPublica — The Secret IRS Files — Analysis of leaked IRS data showing the true income tax burden on the 25 wealthiest Americans relative to wealth growth, 2014–2018.
  • Forbes Real-Time Billionaires — Annual and real-time estimates of individual billionaire net worth used as the primary source for global billionaire wealth totals.

Caveats & Methodology

  • Billionaire wealth estimates (Forbes, Bloomberg) are derived from publicly known asset holdings — listed equity stakes, real estate, and disclosed ownership — and exclude hard-to-value private assets, derivatives, and trust structures, likely understating true net worth.
  • Effective tax rate comparisons across countries conflate structurally different systems: some levy annual wealth taxes (Norway, Spain), others tax only realization events, and some have no capital gains tax at all, making a single cross-national rate figure a simplification.
  • A large share of billionaire wealth consists of unrealized capital gains that face no tax until sold; including or excluding these dramatically changes calculated effective rates, and different studies use different conventions.
